Home page

Welcome to Sunny Days

At Sunny Days, we believe that every person deserves a space where they feel seen, heard, and supported. Life can bring moments of uncertainty, stress, and emotional pain—especially during adolescence and young adulthood. But it can also bring healing, resilience, and growth. Our mission is to help you find your light, even when the skies feel cloudy.

Sunny Days offers a welcoming environment for adolescents, teens, young adults, and families to explore emotions, build coping skills, and rediscover balance. Whether you’re facing challenges with anxiety, school pressures, relationships, or life transitions, you don’t have to face them alone.

We provide both in-person counseling in a comfortable, private setting and secure telehealth sessions for those who prefer flexibility or live outside the local area. Wherever you are, Sunny Days is here to help you take that first step toward emotional wellness and a brighter tomorrow.

Why Choose Sunny Days?

At Sunny Days, therapy is not a one-size-fits-all process—it’s a collaborative journey. Our approach combines empathy, evidence-based strategies, and a deep respect for each client’s individual story. We focus on the whole person—mind, body, and spirit—helping clients build resilience and self-understanding that lasts well beyond the therapy room.

Sunny Days was founded by Rachel Day, M.Ed., M.A., LGPC, LSC, a compassionate counselor with a passion for supporting youth and families through life’s challenges. Rachel’s warm, approachable style creates a safe space where healing and personal growth can flourish.

About

Meet Rachel Day, M.Ed., M.A., LGPC, LSC

Rachel Day is the founder and lead counselor of Sunny Days. She is both a Licensed Graduate Professional Counselor (LGPC) and a Licensed School Counselor (LSC) with extensive experience working with children, adolescents, young adults, and families. Rachel’s dual background in education and clinical counseling gives her a unique understanding of the social, academic, and emotional challenges facing today’s youth.

Rachel’s counseling style is characterized by empathy, warmth, and authenticity. She believes that every person has the inner strength to heal and grow—and that therapy provides the safe, supportive environment where that growth can begin. Her work is guided by the belief that lasting change comes from self-awareness, healthy relationships, and compassion toward oneself.

Currently pursuing her doctorate in psychology, Rachel is deeply committed to ongoing education and to incorporating the latest research and therapeutic approaches into her practice. Her goal is to offer clients the highest quality care, grounded in both science and empathy.

Therapeutic Philosophy

At Sunny Days, we believe that therapy is a collaborative, empowering process. Rachel’s approach integrates cognitive-behavioral, person-centered, and mindfulness-based techniques within a holistic framework. She tailors each session to meet the unique needs of every client—recognizing that each person’s story, pace, and goals are different.

Rachel emphasizes:

Empathy and Trust: Creating a safe, judgment-free environment where clients feel comfortable being their authentic selves.

Collaboration and Growth: Working together to identify goals, build insight, and develop practical strategies for everyday life.

Holistic Wellness: Supporting emotional, academic, social, and physical well-being as interconnected parts of a balanced life.

Resilience and Strength: Helping clients recognize their inner resources and build confidence in their ability to face challenges.

Rachel’s holistic, evidence-based approach helps clients move from feeling overwhelmed or stuck to feeling more grounded, capable, and hopeful about their future.

Services

At Sunny Days, we provide a range of counseling services designed to support individuals and families through every stage of life. Our services are offered in-person and via secure telehealth sessions, allowing clients to choose the format that best fits their comfort and schedule.

Individual Counseling

For adolescents, teens, and young adults

Life can feel overwhelming at times—especially when you’re managing school, friendships, identity, or the transition into adulthood. Individual counseling offers a safe, supportive space to explore emotions, gain insight, and build healthy coping skills.

Common areas of focus include:

Anxiety, depression, and mood regulation

Academic or school-related stress

Self-esteem and self-identity

Life transitions and decision-making

Relationship and communication challenges

Through empathy and evidence-based approaches, Rachel helps clients build confidence, emotional awareness, and resilience.

Family Counseling

Healthy family connections are essential to emotional well-being. Family counseling provides an opportunity for open communication, understanding, and healing. Sessions are designed to strengthen relationships, resolve conflict, and promote collaboration within the family system.

Focus areas may include:

Parent-child communication

Conflict resolution

Adjusting to life changes or transitions

Emotional and behavioral challenges

Building stronger family bonds

Family therapy helps each member feel heard and valued while working together toward shared growth and connection.

School and Academic Support

As a licensed school counselor, Rachel has a deep understanding of the academic and social pressures students face. Sunny Days offers support for students and families navigating educational stress, motivation, and self-advocacy.

Services may include:

Academic stress management

Organization and executive functioning skills

School transitions (elementary to middle, middle to high school, high school to college)

Consultation with parents or educators on student well-being

Telehealth Counseling

For clients who prefer flexibility or live outside the local area, telehealth counseling offers the same high-quality, confidential care through a secure online platform. Whether you’re balancing a busy schedule or feel most comfortable at home, virtual sessions can make therapy more accessible and convenient.
